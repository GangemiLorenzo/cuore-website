Getting a good reading

Cuore uses your iPhone's rear camera and flash to measure your pulse through your fingertip. For the most accurate results:

  • Rest your fingertip gently on the camera lens — don't press hard.
  • Cover both the camera lens and the flash completely.
  • Sit still and avoid talking during the 30-second measurement.
  • Good lighting conditions help, but the flash itself provides the light needed.
  • Take measurements when rested — right after exercise your heart rate will still be elevated.

The app says "no finger detected"

This usually means your finger isn't covering the camera fully, or you're pressing too hard and restricting blood flow. Try repositioning so your fingertip sits flat over both the camera and flash without applying pressure.

My reading seems inaccurate

Cuore uses photoplethysmography (PPG) — the same optical technology used in most smartwatches. Results are estimates and can be affected by movement, cold hands, or poor finger placement. If readings seem consistently off, try a few measurements in a row while sitting still in a warm room.

Cuore is a wellness app, not a medical device. Results should not be used to diagnose or monitor any medical condition.

Apple Health isn't saving

Make sure Cuore has permission to write to Apple Health. Go to Settings → Privacy & Security → Health → Cuore and ensure Heart Rate and Heart Rate Variability are enabled.

Restoring a Pro subscription

If you've reinstalled the app or switched devices, tap Restore Purchases on the paywall screen. Make sure you're signed into the same Apple ID used for the original purchase.

Cancelling or managing your subscription

Subscriptions are managed entirely through Apple. To cancel or view your billing: go to Settings → [your name] → Subscriptions on your iPhone.

Deleting your data

All measurement history is stored locally on your device — we hold no data on external servers. To delete everything, go to Settings → General → iPhone Storage → Cuore → Delete App. You can also clear individual measurements from the History tab inside the app.
